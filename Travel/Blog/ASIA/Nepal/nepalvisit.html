The Greater Nepal, Nepal

Many Beautiful Things to Do in Nepal in Summer + Hidden Gems & Events

Updated on August 20, 2023 by Travel Abroad
Previous Next
"Greater Nepal" refers to the historical concept of an expanded Nepalese territory that included areas beyond its current borders, extending into regions like Sikkim, Bhutan, and parts of northern India. This idea was prominent during Nepal's expansionist period in the 18th and early 19th centuries.
Visiting Nepal offers a captivating blend of breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural experiences, and spiritual exploration. From the majestic Himalayas and serene valleys to vibrant cities and ancient temples, Nepal offers:
1. Himalayan Beauty: Experience awe-inspiring views of the world's highest peaks, including Mount Everest. Trekking and mountaineering opportunities abound, letting you explore lush trails, remote villages, and stunning vistas.
2. Cultural Diversity: Immerse yourself in diverse cultures, languages, and traditions as you interact with the warm and welcoming Nepalese people. Explore ancient cities like Kathmandu and Bhaktapur to witness historic temples, palaces, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
3. Spiritual Retreats: Nepal is the birthplace of Buddha and home to numerous monasteries and spiritual sites. Discover inner peace through meditation, yoga, or simply by visiting sacred places like Lumbini.
4. Adventure Activities: Engage in thrilling activities like white-water rafting, paragliding, and jungle safaris in national parks to encounter exotic wildlife like tigers and rhinos.
5. Local Cuisine: Savor a variety of authentic Nepalese dishes, from momos (dumplings) to dal bhat (lentil soup with rice). Enjoy the culinary journey through flavors unique to the region.
6. Friendly Locals: Interact with locals who are eager to share their culture, stories, and insights, creating meaningful connections and lasting memories.
7. Handicrafts and Souvenirs: Explore local markets for unique handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs that showcase Nepal's artistic heritage.
8. Natural Beauty: Discover lush forests, serene lakes, and picturesque landscapes that showcase Nepal's unparalleled natural beauty.
9. Adventure and Tranquility: Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or a blend of both, Nepal's diverse offerings cater to various travel preferences.
10. Philanthropic Opportunities: Many travelers engage in volunteer work or support local initiatives, contributing positively to Nepal's communities and development.
Visiting Nepal is an enriching experience that offers a balance between adventure, spirituality, culture, and breathtaking landscapes, making it a destination unlike any other.

Many Beautiful Things to Do at Chameli Mai Temple in Summer + Hidden Gems & Events

Previous Next
Chameli Mai Mandir is a prominent Hindu temple located in Sarlahi, Nepal. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Chameli, who is revered as a manifestation of the goddess Durga. The temple holds significant religious importance for locals and attracts visitors for several reasons.
People visit Chameli Mai Mandir to seek blessings, offer prayers, and perform rituals to Goddess Chameli, who is believed to protect devotees from various hardships and challenges. The temple's serene and spiritual atmosphere provides a place for reflection and devotion.
The architecture and historical significance of the temple also draw visitors. Many are captivated by the intricate carvings, colorful decorations, and unique architectural style that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region.
Festivals and events held at the temple are another reason for its popularity. During festivals like Navaratri, Dashain, and Tihar, the temple becomes a focal point for celebrations, drawing devotees from different parts of Nepal to participate in the festivities.
Overall, Chameli Mai Mandir's religious importance, architectural beauty, and cultural events make it a cherished destination for pilgrims and tourists alike.

Birgunj, a bustling city located in Nepal near the Indian border, offers a diverse array of attractions for visitors to explore. From cultural landmarks to natural beauty, Birgunj has something for everyone. Whether you're a history enthusiast, a nature lover, or someone seeking a taste of local life, this city has much to offer. Let's delve into some of the notable places you can visit in Birgunj:

1. Maisthan Temple: A revered religious site dedicated to Goddess Rakheshwari Devi, the Maisthan Temple is a beautiful example of traditional Nepali and Indian architecture. The temple's intricate carvings and vibrant colors make it a must-visit for those interested in cultural and spiritual experiences.


2. Clock Tower: A prominent landmark in Birgunj, the Clock Tower stands tall in the heart of the city. It's not just a timekeeping structure; it's a symbol of the city's history and growth.


3. Shankaracharya Gate: This impressive gate marks the entry point to Birgunj from the Indian side. It's named after the revered philosopher Adi Shankaracharya and serves as a symbolic connection between the two countries.


4. Bindabasini Temple: Another important religious site, the Bindabasini Temple, is dedicated to Goddess Durga. The temple's serene ambiance and spiritual significance attract both locals and tourists alike.


5. irgunj Haat Bazaar: For a glimpse into the local way of life, Birgunj's Haat Bazaar offers a bustling market where you can shop for traditional handicrafts, clothing, and local produce.


6. Gandak Barrage: Nature enthusiasts will appreciate a visit to the Gandak Barrage, a hydraulic structure built on the Gandak River. It's a great spot for birdwatching and taking in views of the river and surrounding landscapes.


7. Kali Mandir: This temple dedicated to Goddess Kali is another important religious site in Birgunj. It's particularly vibrant during festivals when devotees gather to pay their respects.


8. Ghadiwarba Pokhari: "Ghadiwarba Pokhari" in Birgunj refers to a historical pond located in the heart of the city. The term "Ghadiwarba" translates to "Clock Tower" in English, while "Pokhari" means "pond" or "lake." The Ghadiwarba Pokhari is situated near the Clock Tower, which is a prominent landmark in Birgunj.
This pond holds significance as a historical and cultural site, serving as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike. Over the years, it has become a tranquil spot where people can relax, enjoy the surroundings, and perhaps feed the fish that inhabit the pond. The combination of the clock tower, the pond, and the surrounding area creates a unique and picturesque atmosphere in the heart of the city.
The Ghadiwarba Pokhari's location near the Clock Tower adds to its charm, as it becomes a part of the city's narrative and history. It's not only a place of natural beauty but also a representation of Birgunj's growth and development over time.

As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, Bagmati Province is one of the seven provinces in Nepal, located in the central part of the country. However, please note that there might have been changes or developments since then, so I recommend checking with more recent sources to get the most up-to-date information.
Bagmati Province, particularly the Kathmandu Valley area, offers a unique and rich cultural, historical, and natural experience that makes it a compelling destination for travelers. Here are some reasons why you might consider visiting Bagmati Province:
1. Capital and Major Cities: The capital of Bagmati Province is Hetauda. Other major cities in the province include Kathmandu (the national capital of Nepal), Lalitpur, and Bhaktapur.
2. Geography: Bagmati Province is situated in the central part of Nepal and is characterized by diverse topography, ranging from the flatlands of the Terai region to the hilly and mountainous areas of the Mahabharat and Sivalik ranges.
3. Cultural Significance: The province is home to the Kathmandu Valley, which holds immense cultural and historical significance. The valley is renowned for its ancient temples, palaces, and traditional Newari architecture. UNESCO has designated several sites in the valley as World Heritage Sites.
4. Economy: The province's economy is diverse, with agriculture, trade, tourism, and services being significant contributors. The Kathmandu Valley, in particular, serves as a hub for commerce, administration, and tourism.
5. Ethnic Diversity: Bagmati Province is home to a diverse range of ethnic groups and communities. The Newars, an indigenous ethnic group, have a strong presence in the Kathmandu Valley and are known for their distinct culture, art, and festivals.
6. Tourism: The province is a major tourist destination in Nepal due to its cultural heritage, historical sites, and natural beauty. Kathmandu, Bhaktapur, and Lalitpur are popular for their architectural marvels, while areas around the province offer trekking, hiking, and adventure tourism opportunities.
7. Education and Research: The province hosts numerous educational institutions, including universities, colleges, and schools. Kathmandu, in particular, is a center for education, research, and academia.
8. Local Governance: With Nepal transitioning from a unitary system to a federal structure, Bagmati Province has its own provincial government responsible for various administrative and governance functions within the province.
Kathmandu, Nepal

Many Beautiful Things to Do in Kathmandu in Summer + Hidden Gems & Events

Previous Next
1. A visit to Pashupatinath Temple is a profound and spiritual experience. The temple's ornate architecture, vibrant rituals, and serene atmosphere create a sense of reverence. Removing shoes at the entrance, the scent of incense, and the sound of chanting add to the sacred ambiance. Inside the main temple, devotees offer prayers to the statue of Lord Shiva. Along the Bagmati River's ghats, various rituals unfold, including cremations that symbolize the cycle of life and death. Interactions with sadhus provide insight into the temple's spiritual significance. As the sun sets, the evening aarti ceremony captivates with lamps, singing, and ringing bells. Leaving the temple, visitors carry a deep connection to Hindu spirituality and a lasting impression of the temple's energy and devotion.
2. Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple): Perched atop a hill, Swayambhunath is an iconic stupa with a striking white dome and a watchful pair of eyes painted on its pinnacle. The complex is not only a religious site for Buddhists but also offers panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley.
3. Boudhanath Stupa: One of the largest stupas in Nepal, Boudhanath features a massive mandala with the eyes of Buddha gazing out. This Tibetan Buddhist site is a hub of religious activities, and its serene ambiance is a haven for meditation and reflection.
4. Kathmandu Durbar Square: This historic square is dotted with ancient palaces, courtyards, and temples that showcase intricate Newari architecture. The Kumari Ghar (home of the Living Goddess) is a prominent highlight.
5. Patan Durbar Square: Located in the city of Lalitpur, this square is known for its well-preserved palace and artistic courtyards. The Patan Museum within the square houses a rich collection of traditional Nepali art.
6. Bhaktapur Durbar Square: This UNESCO World Heritage Site in Bhaktapur features an array of temples, palaces, and intricately designed buildings. The square provides a glimpse into Newari culture and architecture.
7. Thamel: A bustling neighborhood in Kathmandu, Thamel is known for its vibrant atmosphere, shops, restaurants, and accommodations catering to tourists. It's a popular place to explore, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy local cuisine.
8. Garden of Dreams: This serene oasis in the heart of Kathmandu offers a peaceful escape from the city's bustle. With its well-manicured gardens, fountains, and neoclassical architecture, it's a great spot for relaxation.
9. Narayanhiti Palace Museum: Once the royal palace, this museum offers insights into Nepal's history and monarchy. Visitors can explore the grand halls and rooms while learning about the country's royal past.
10. Rani Pokhari: This historic artificial pond in central Kathmandu has cultural significance and is surrounded by a tranquil park. It's a popular spot for locals and visitors alike.
11. Dakshinkali Temple: Located just outside Kathmandu, this temple is dedicated to the goddess Kali. It's a pilgrimage site and known for animal sacrifices during certain festivals.
12. Chandragiri Hill: For panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley and the surrounding mountains, visitors can take a cable car ride to the top of Chandragiri Hill.
13. National Museum (Rashtriya Museum): Located in Chhauni, this museum offers a comprehensive collection of artifacts that showcase Nepal's art, culture, history, and natural heritage.
14. Asan Bazaar: This bustling market area is known for its colorful shops selling spices, textiles, handicrafts, and other local products. It's a vibrant place to experience the local lifestyle.
15. Goddess Kumari's Residence: In Kathmandu Durbar Square, you can catch a glimpse of the Kumari, the Living Goddess of Nepal, when she appears in her window. The Kumari Ghar is her residence.
16. Hanuman Dhoka Durbar Square: Another historic square in Kathmandu, this area is named after the statue of Hanuman (Monkey God) near the entrance. It's surrounded by palaces, courtyards, and temples.
17. Indra Chowk: This bustling square in the heart of Kathmandu is known for its traditional market and vibrant street life. It's a great place to experience the local culture.
18. Gokarna Mahadev Temple: Located in the Gokarna Forest, this temple is surrounded by a serene woodland area. It's a peaceful spot for reflection and a peaceful walk.
19. Chhangu Narayan Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu is located on a hilltop near Bhaktapur. It's known for its intricate carvings and panoramic views.
20. Dhulikhel: A short drive from Kathmandu, Dhulikhel offers stunning Himalayan views and a peaceful atmosphere. It's a great place for a day trip or a short getaway.
21. Kirtipur: This historic town south of Kathmandu offers traditional Newari architecture, narrow alleys, and a view tower that provides panoramic views of the surrounding area.
22. Langtang National Park: While not in Kathmandu itself, the park is accessible from the city and offers trekking opportunities through beautiful landscapes, rhododendron forests, and picturesque villages.
23. Godavari Botanical Garden: Located at the base of Phulchowki Hill, this garden is known for its diverse plant species, tranquil ponds, and peaceful walking trails.
24. Patan Industrial Estate: This area is known for its traditional handicrafts and workshops where you can observe artisans at work and purchase authentic Nepali crafts.
25. Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park: North of Kathmandu, this park offers hiking trails and the opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the area. The Nagarjun Hill provides panoramic views.
26. Shechen Monastery: This Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Boudhanath is known for its vibrant colors and traditional architecture. It's also a center for Buddhist teachings and practices.
27. Chobhar: Located southwest of Kathmandu, Chobhar is known for its gorge and the temple of Adinath. The Manjushree Park offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and the valley.
28. Taudaha Lake: This serene lake located on the outskirts of Kathmandu is a great spot for birdwatching and picnics. It's a peaceful retreat away from the city's hustle.
29. Shri Panchayan Mandir (Changu Narayan Temple): A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ancient temple complex showcases intricate wood and stone carvings and offers insights into Nepal's history and art.
30. The Garden of Six Seasons (Hariharbhawan): This garden in Lalitpur offers a diverse range of plants, flowers, and sculptures, making it a pleasant place for nature lovers.
31. Patan Dhoka (Patan Gate): This historic gate marks the entrance to the city of Lalitpur and is an impressive example of traditional Newari architecture.
32. Tribhuvan University Campus: Take a stroll through the sprawling campus of Nepal's oldest and largest university, situated in Kirtipur. It has a mix of old and modern buildings.
33. Guheshwari Temple: Located near Pashupatinath Temple, this temple is dedicated to the goddess Guheshwari and holds significance for both Hindus and Buddhists.
34. Bajrabarahi: A popular pilgrimage site located south of Kathmandu, Bajrabarahi is known for its temple and beautiful hilltop views.
35. Nagarkot: A bit farther from Kathmandu, Nagarkot is famous for its sunrise and sunset views over the Himalayas. It's a popular destination for a peaceful getaway.
36. Daksheswari Temple: Situated near Pashupatinath, this temple complex is dedicated to the goddess Daksheswari and is an important Hindu pilgrimage site.
37. Budhanilkantha Temple: This temple is known for its large statue of Lord Vishnu lying on a bed of snakes. It's a significant Hindu religious site.
38. White Monastery (Seto Gumba): Located on a hilltop in Swayambhu, this monastery offers panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley and a peaceful environment for meditation.
39. Shankha Park: A recreational park in the heart of Kathmandu, Shankha Park is a relaxing place for a leisurely stroll or to enjoy some downtime.
Pokhara , Gandaki, Nepal

Many Beautiful Things to Do in Pokhara, Nepal in Summer + Hidden Gems & Events

Previous Next
1. Phewa Lake: The star attraction of Pokhara, Phewa Lake is a serene body of water surrounded by lush hills and the Annapurna range in the background. Boating on the lake offers breathtaking views and a chance to explore the Tal Barahi Temple situated on an island in the lake.
2. Sarangkot: A popular viewpoint just outside Pokhara, Sarangkot offers stunning panoramic views of the sunrise and the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. Paragliding from Sarangkot is also a popular adventure activity.
3. World Peace Pagoda: Situated on a hilltop, this gleaming white stupa offers panoramic views of the city, the lake, and the surrounding mountains. It's a peaceful place for reflection and meditation.
4. Davis Falls: Also known as Patale Chhango, this waterfall originates from the Phewa Lake and cascades into a deep gorge. It's an impressive sight, and a visit to the cave behind the falls is an adventurous experience.
5. Gupteshwor Cave: Located near Davis Falls, this cave is known for its intricate rock formations and a shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. Exploring the cave provides an interesting glimpse into the natural formations.
6. Mahendra Cave: Another cave attraction in Pokhara, Mahendra Cave features limestone formations and underground chambers that you can explore with the help of guides.
7. Bindabasini Temple: This Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Bhagwati is situated on a hill and offers panoramic views of the city and the Himalayas. It's an important religious site for locals.
8. Seti River Gorge: The Seti River has carved a deep gorge through the city. You can witness the milky white waters flowing through the narrow chasm from various vantage points, including a bridge.
9. International Mountain Museum: This museum showcases Nepal's mountaineering history, cultural diversity, and the flora and fauna of the Himalayas. It's a great place to learn about the world's highest peaks.
10. Pokhara Regional Museum: This museum provides insights into the cultural heritage and history of the region. It features artifacts, traditional art, and exhibits about the Gurkha soldiers.
11. Begnas Lake: Located a bit farther from Phewa Lake, Begnas Lake is a quieter and less touristy alternative. The pristine surroundings and calm waters make it a great place for relaxation.
12. Rupa Lake: Adjacent to Begnas Lake, Rupa Lake offers similar tranquility and scenic beauty. It's a peaceful spot for boating and birdwatching.
13. Shanti Stupa: Similar to the World Peace Pagoda, Shanti Stupa offers panoramic views and a serene atmosphere. It's a place for quiet reflection and enjoying the natural beauty.
14. Tal Barahi Temple: This pagoda-style temple is situated on an island in Phewa Lake. It's dedicated to the goddess Barahi and is accessible by boat.
15. Pokhara Zoo: If you're interested in wildlife, the zoo offers a chance to see various species of animals and birds native to Nepal.
